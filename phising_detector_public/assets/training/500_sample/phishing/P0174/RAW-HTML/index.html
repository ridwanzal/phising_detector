Late Term Abortion

Is it still legal to get a late-term abortion?

The majority of states in the U.S. restrict abortion after fetal viability, third trimester, or after a predetermined number of weeks established by state law.Some states also allow exceptions for health of the mother (and sometimes fetal abnormality) are generally made.

I am too far along for an abortion, what now?

Our clinic specializes in helping women with unplanned pregnancies. Call and schedule your appointment today to see what we can do for you.

How much does a late term abortion cost?

The cost of a late term abortion is significantly higher. It is not unusual for a late term abortion to cost $10,000 or more. Many times the cost is decided on a case by case basis between the woman and the clinic.

Will my insurance cover my abortion?

Abortion coverage by insurance depends on state laws and insurance policies. Call your insurance provider for details.

Does Medicaid cover abortion?

The majority of states in the U.S. follow federal standards of only funding abortion in cases involving life endangerment, rape, and incest.

Abortion Options

What are my options?

The type of abortion available to you will depend on gestational age and preference:

  • Medical abortion- up to nine weeks after a missed period
  • Dilatation and curettage with vacuum aspiration- up to twelve weeks after a missed period
  • Dilatation and evacuation- after twelve weeks after a missed period
  • Abortion by Labor Induction- after sixteen weeks of a missed period
  • Adoption
  • Parenting

At what stage of pregnancy can abortion be restricted?

The majority of states in the U.S. restrict abortions after viability, 20 weeks, or 24 weeks.

How much does a late term abortion cost?

The cost of a late term abortion is significantly higher. It is not unusual for a late term abortion to cost $10,000 or more. Many times the cost is decided on a case by case basis between the woman and the clinic.

Where can I get an abortion?

Abortions are typically performed at a health care provider’s office, a clinic, or hospital depending on the type of procedure, how far along you are, and your health.

What is the difference between medical and surgical abortion?

A medical abortion is a series of pills taken to cause the pregnancy to end. A surgical abortion requires invasive procedures by a physician to remove the pregnancy.

I am pregnant. Is there financial assistance available to me?

There are several agencies that offer assistance for pregnant women. Call and schedule an appointment with us today to learn more about your options.
